Coders in the Newsroom

What is different working in a newsroom than developing software in a startup or company?

By Gabriela Rodriguez / @gaba

There is usually programmers and other technical professionals working in the newsrooms, maintaining the servers and designing the website. The difference with what is happening now is that many coders are coming to work directly inside the newsroom collaborating with the journalists face to face. The goal is always to tell a story

Free Software

many newsrooms with public repositories and pushing for collaboration

backbone.js, django came out of the work at newsrooms

Open Source is arriving to the newsrooms. Many newsrooms and journalists are collaborating with each other and opening their projects. NYT, La Nacion and others have repos in github showing their code. Many interesting technology came out of newsrooms like backbone (from the work on DocumentCloud) and Django.

Exciting Projects!

Exciting and interesting projects come up from the newsrooms. You partner with the journalists to research a specific topic and end up learning a lot about it. You learn a lot about many topics!

Space Open for Experimentation

- wide open space right now. There is a lot of space for experimentation. - What do journalists need to make their work better? How can they learn to analyze data. - Foundations like the Knight-Foundation giving grands for journalism and technology

The tools you build are being used

- people use what you do - you work with the journalist in tools and websites to analyze data and tell stories

We need you!

- More voices and ways to create journalism make it more powerful and a real reflection on what is going on in our communities - And journalism needs more diversity in voices. The same as with technology

Technology and Journalism are shaping the world!

- Journalism And Technology are shaping the world - journalism shape how we view the world - Technology and data are not neutral - Technology in media can influence on how we are looking at the world

How did I arrive to Journalism?

Not trusting "traditional media"

through community and independent media and activism

- In 2001 there was a big economic crisis in Uruguay and Argentina. The traditional media was not showing what we were seeing was happening in the streets. There was the need for a media that we could trust. We started to make media in the internet and came up with the motto "everybody is a journalist". The idea that journalism feeds from the community that it serves. - Mozilla Open News - With that experience in mind I came to be very very excited about the work that The Coral Project is doing to put in the table a discussion on how we integrate the readers into the journalism that is made in the newsrooms
